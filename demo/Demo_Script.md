# MAI Inspector - Official Build Week Demo

Version 2.0

Production Script

Duration: approximately 2 minutes 45 seconds

Objective: demonstrate how MAI Inspector moves AI from document understanding to decision assessment.

## Scene 1 - The Problem

Time: 0:00-0:20

### Narration

Every day, people make important decisions based on presentations, reports, technical documents, financial models, and due diligence packages.

AI can summarize and explain these documents remarkably well.

But one critical question often remains unanswered:

Do we actually have enough evidence to responsibly make this decision?

### On Screen

Large centered text.

```text
AI understands information.
```

Pause 1 second.

```text
People still do not know whether they have enough evidence to act.

MAI Inspector fills that gap.
```

Hold for two seconds.

## Scene 2 - Proposed Decision

Time: 0:20-0:35

### Narration

Imagine an investor evaluating a new opportunity.

MAI Inspector begins with the decision, not the documents.

### On Screen

```text
Proposed Decision

Should we proceed with an investment commitment based on the current evidence package?
```

### Action

Open a new assessment.

Display the Proposed Decision.

Pause briefly before loading the evidence.

## Scene 3 - Evidence Package

Time: 0:35-0:50

### Narration

The investor provides the currently available evidence:

- company presentation
- technical documentation
- financial information
- supporting reports

The question is not whether these documents are interesting.

The question is whether they are sufficient to support the proposed commitment.

### On Screen

```text
Evidence Package

Presentation.pdf
Technical Report.pdf
Financial Model.xlsx
Supporting Report.pdf
```

Documents are uploaded.

## Scene 4 - OpenAI Semantic Layer

Time: 0:50-1:15

### Narration

The OpenAI model analyzes the evidence package.

It identifies material claims, structures the supporting evidence, detects contradictions, and connects the available information to the proposed decision.

At this stage, unstructured documents become structured decision evidence.

### On Screen

```text
Documents
      v
Material Claims
      v
Supporting Evidence
      v
Decision Context
```

Lower caption:

```text
OpenAI
Semantic understanding and evidence structuring
```

## Scene 5 - MAI Decision Intelligence Engine

Time: 1:15-1:45

### Narration

The structured evidence is then evaluated by the deterministic MAI Decision Intelligence Engine.

The roles are deliberately separated.

OpenAI structures and interprets the evidence.

MAI applies reproducible assessment logic and establishes the decision boundary.

The purpose is not to replace human judgment.

It is to assess what level of commitment the available evidence can responsibly support.

### On Screen

```text
OpenAI
Evidence understanding
and structuring
        v
MAI Decision Intelligence Engine
Deterministic
Evidence-to-Commitment Assessment
```

Right side:

```text
[x] Evidence Sufficiency
[x] Consistency
[x] Missing Evidence
[x] Commitment Boundary
```

## Scene 6 - Executive Decision Assessment

Time: 1:45-2:25

### Narration

The assessment produces five executive outputs.

### On Screen

```text
Executive Decision Assessment

Decision Status
NOT YET
```

No narration.

No camera movement.

No animation.

Then reveal:

```text
Highest Responsible Commitment
STRUCTURED DUE DILIGENCE
```

Reveal remaining results:

```text
Critical Blockers
- Independent technical validation missing
- Financial verification incomplete
- Commercial assumptions insufficiently supported

Required Evidence
- Independent technical validation
- Verified financial information
- Commercial and operational evidence

Recommended Next Step
Proceed with Structured Due Diligence before considering an investment commitment.
```

### Narration After All Five Outputs Appear

The proposed investment commitment is not yet supported.

But the available evidence does support the next responsible step:

Structured Due Diligence.

## Scene 7 - Closing

Time: 2:25-2:45

### Narration

This is the central distinction.

MAI Inspector does not simply analyze documents.

It assesses what level of commitment those documents responsibly support.

### Final Screen

```text
From Document Intelligence

v

to

v

Decision Intelligence
```

Pause.

Fade.

### Final Brand Screen

```text
MAI Inspector
Decision Intelligence for High-Stakes Decisions
```

### Final Narration

MAI Inspector helps people understand what their evidence responsibly allows them to do next.

Fade to black.

## Production Notes

### Voice

- Calm
- Executive
- Engineering confidence
- No marketing tone

### Visual Style

- Clean white interface
- Dark typography
- Minimal animation
- Focus attention on assessment results
- Avoid decorative transitions

## Timing

The most important timing in the entire video:

| Screen | Pause |
|---|---:|
| Decision Status -> NOT YET | 2 sec |
| Highest Responsible Commitment -> STRUCTURED DUE DILIGENCE | 2 sec |

Those pauses are deliberate. They allow the audience to absorb the conclusion before additional information appears.

## Final Message

If the reviewer watches the video without sound, they should still understand the product's core value:

- A decision was proposed.
- Evidence was analyzed.
- The system did not reject the opportunity.
- The system identified the highest responsible commitment currently supported by the evidence.
- It explained what is missing and what should happen next.

If the video communicates those five points visually, it demonstrates the distinguishing capability of MAI Inspector far more effectively than verbal explanation alone.

This version is the production baseline for the Build Week submission.
